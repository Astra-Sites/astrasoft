Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the Astra Scholars AI Fellowship

The Astra Scholars AI Fellowship is an intensive 8-week program designed to transform beginners into professional AI engineers. You'll learn to build real-world AI applications using modern tools and APIs, culminating in a capstone project and physical demo day in Nairobi.

We're looking for motivated individuals with basic programming knowledge (any language) and a passion for AI. No prior AI experience required. This program is perfect for developers, students, and career changers who want to break into the AI engineering field.

  • Basic computer literacy and internet access
  • A computer (Windows, Mac, or Linux)
  • 10+ hours per week commitment
  • Ability to attend the final Nairobi Demo Day
  • English proficiency for communication

The Astra Scholars AI Fellowship has a one-time fee of KES 25,000 (approximately $194 USD). This covers mentorship, curriculum access, project reviews, certification, and the Nairobi Demo Day event. Payment plans are available.

You'll master modern AI engineering skills including:
  • API integration with Gemini and OpenAI
  • Building custom AI agents and chatbots
  • Server-side programming and automation
  • Project deployment and scaling
  • Career development and freelancing skills

Graduates typically secure roles as AI Engineers, Machine Learning Engineers, or Backend Developers. Many start freelancing on platforms like Upwork, building AI-powered applications. Our alumni have landed positions at tech companies and started their own AI consulting businesses.

Applications are now open! Click the "Apply for Fellowship" button above to fill out our application form. We'll review your background and motivation, then invite qualified candidates for a brief interview. The application process takes about 15 minutes.

You'll have direct access to senior AI engineers through our mentorship program. We also have active community channels on Discord and WhatsApp for peer support. Office hours are held twice weekly, and you can schedule 1-on-1 sessions with mentors as needed.
